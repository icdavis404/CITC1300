Military Aircraft

Types of Military Aircraft

There are numerous types of miltary aircraft, each of which serve its own specific role and purpose. Fighter planes such as the F-14 Tomcat are primarily designed for air to air combat, although many are designed to fulfill multiple roles. One such multirole fighter is the F-35 Lightning II. Cargo planes such as the C-130 Hercules and C-5 Galaxy transport personnel and equipment across the globe.

Military aircraft have both a name and a designation. The designations are made up of letters defining the primary role of the aircraft, as well as a number. Aircraft with the F designation are fighter aircraft. Those with the A designation are designed for attacking ground targets. Some planes are designed for electronic warfare and will likely be equipped with a radar or other electronic countermeasures. As a final example, the SR-71 was designed for reconnaisance and was not equipped with any weapons. Its defence against other aircraft and surface to air weapons was its speed; it was able to achieve speeds upwards of Mach 3.2.
